Classifying e-commerce products based on images and text

Sun 26 June 2016

During my time at Insight I consulted for a Y-combinator startup Lynks that allows people in Egypt to ship fashion and fashion related items from US e-commerce stores to their homes in Egypt. This is a useful service as most of the US e-commerce website do not ship their products to the middle East. Lynks doesn't have it's own inventory and its theoretical inventory is the combined one of all US based e-commerce stores. All the products that are ordered have been classified by hand and Lynks has asked me to help prototype a pipeline that can assist in automating this task. In the best case scenario this work will contribute to a fully automated classification pipeline, lower hanging fruit is to assist the human labelers while more data is collected and the predictive model gets improved. Given that Lynks has been doing this by hand they have a decently sized data labeled data set. The data currently collected for each product is an image and a short description of the item.
